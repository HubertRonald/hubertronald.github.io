# Product Vision

VersoVector starts from poetry because poetry concentrates emotion, metaphor, rhythm, ambiguity, memory, and symbolic language.

The long-term product vision is to evolve from poetry embeddings into an emotional-semantic recommendation engine for poetic and lyrical content.

## Core product question

> Can a system help users discover texts that feel emotionally, semantically, or symbolically close?

The goal is not only to recommend similar texts, but to explain why they feel close.

## Target experience

A user provides:

- a poem;
- a lyric-like fragment;
- a reflective paragraph;
- a short emotional text;
- a curated item from a licensed catalog.

The system returns:

- emotional or thematic tags;
- semantic neighbors;
- topic signals;
- cluster assignment;
- similarity explanations;
- visualization metadata;
- recommended related works.

## Potential use cases

VersoVector may support:

- literary exploration;
- poetry discovery;
- mood-aware reading;
- lyric-like content analysis;
- music discovery interfaces;
- educational tools;
- library and archive enrichment;
- editorial metadata enrichment;
- ecommerce catalog enrichment for books, music, or creative content.

## Public vs production scope

The public repository demonstrates the architecture and modeling direction.

The production version may later provide:

- curated datasets;
- licensed content integrations;
- user accounts;
- saved analysis sessions;
- recommendation APIs;
- private model serving;
- commercial-grade UI;
- analytics;
- content governance.

## Product principle

VersoVector should feel:

- calm;
- reflective;
- emotionally intelligent;
- technically credible;
- explainable;
- respectful of content rights.
